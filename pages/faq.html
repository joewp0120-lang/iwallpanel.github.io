1. How to Install WPC Wall Panels

Applies to: interior walls, feature walls, and commercial projects

WPC (Wood Plastic Composite) wall panels are designed for fast, clean installation compared to traditional ‚Äúwet‚Äù methods like plaster and paint. Below is a practical step-by-step guide used by many of our customers worldwide.

Step 1: Check and prepare the wall

  • Ensure the wall is structurally sound, dry, and free of loose dust or peeling paint.
  • For very uneven walls, install wooden or metal battens first so the panels sit flat.
  • For bathrooms or semi-outdoor areas, confirm there is no active water leakage.

Step 2: Plan the layout

  • Measure wall height and width, then calculate how many panels you need, including 5‚Äì10% extra for cutting loss.
  • Decide where you want the first full panel to start so cuts are hidden in corners or behind furniture.
  • Leave small expansion gaps (typically 3‚Äì5 mm) at the edges, especially in areas with temperature changes.

Step 3: Cut panels to size

  • Use a fine-tooth circular saw or table saw for clean edges.
  • Always cut from the back side if you are worried about chipping the decorative surface.

Step 4: Fixing method ‚Äì adhesive, nails, or clips

  • On flat concrete or existing walls: use a strong construction adhesive in ‚ÄúS‚Äù or zigzag pattern on the back of the panel, then press firmly into place.
  • On batten systems: fix panels with brad nails, screws, or hidden clips into the battens.
  • Check level with a spirit level after every 2‚Äì3 panels to keep lines straight.

Step 5: Join panels and finish edges

  • Most WPC panels have tongue-and-groove or click profiles; insert the next panel firmly into the previous one.
  • Use matching profiles or corner trims to finish corners, door edges, and the top/bottom edges for a premium look.

Step 6: Cleaning and maintenance

  • After installation, wipe panels with a soft cloth and mild detergent.
  • No repainting is required; simply clean periodically to maintain the finish.

2. WPC vs. PVC Wall Panels: Which Is Better for High-Humidity Areas?

Applies to: bathrooms, kitchens, basements, coastal regions, and tropical climates

Both WPC and PVC wall panels are popular in modern projects, but they behave differently in high-humidity environments. Understanding the differences helps you choose the right material for each space.

What is WPC?

  • WPC (Wood Plastic Composite) combines plastic with wood fibers.
  • It has a warm, wood-like look and good rigidity.
  • Commonly used in living rooms, bedrooms, offices, and semi-humid areas.

What is PVC?

  • PVC panels are made from pure Polyvinyl Chloride without wood fiber.
  • They are lightweight, fully waterproof, and very easy to clean.
  • Ideal for bathrooms, kitchens, laundry rooms, and other wet areas.

Performance in high humidity

  • Water resistance: PVC is 100% waterproof; WPC is highly resistant but still contains wood fiber, so long-term soaking should be avoided.
  • Dimensional stability: Both materials are stable, but WPC can expand slightly more with temperature and moisture changes, so expansion gaps are important.
  • Mold and mildew: PVC is non-porous and very resistant to mold; WPC performs well when properly installed and ventilated.

Which should you choose?

  • For very wet areas such as showers, public restrooms, and coastal bathrooms, PVC wall panels are usually the safer choice.
  • For semi-humid areas like hotel lobbies, corridors, and office walls in tropical climates, WPC panels offer a warmer, more luxurious appearance while still handling humidity well.
  • Many projects combine both: PVC inside bathrooms, WPC in bedrooms and living spaces for a consistent design language.
